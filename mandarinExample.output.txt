The third-generation leader of the Communist Party of China is 96 years old
Mainland official media announced yesterday that former President Jiang Zemin died in Shanghai at 12:13 noon on the same day at the age of 96 due to leukemia and multiple organ failure. The letter to the whole party, the whole army and the people of all ethnic groups in the country described Jiang Zemin as an outstanding leader recognized by the people and enjoying high prestige, a great Marxist, an outstanding leader of the great cause of socialism with Chinese characteristics, and the third generation of the party's central leadership collective The core of the "Three Represents" and the main founder of the important thought of "Three Represents".
State media announced Jiang's death on Wednesday afternoon
State media announced the news of Jiang Zemin's death at 4:30 p.m. on Wednesday. Subsequently, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress, the State Council, the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference, and the Central Military Commission issued a letter to the entire party, the army, and the people of all ethnic groups in the country. The last time a leader was awarded such a standard was when Deng Xiaoping passed away, and Jiang Zemin also became the 10th recipient of the title of "Great Marxist". According to the announcement, Jiang Zemin worked in a factory after graduating from National Jiaotong University (now called Shanghai Jiaotong University) in 1947. He engaged in revolutionary propaganda work among the workers and professional youths in the night school of the Youth Association. In 1949, he organized the workers to carry out activities to protect the factory. Welcome to the liberation of Shanghai.
In the early days of the founding of the People's Republic of China, Jiang Zemin successively served as the first deputy director of Shanghai Yimin Food No. 1 Factory. In 1955, he went to Moscow Stalin Automobile Factory in the Soviet Union for an internship. In 1980, he served as the deputy director and secretary-general of the State Import and Export Management Committee and the State Foreign Investment Management Committee, and a member of the party group. He was in charge of the implementation of special policies, flexible measures, and pilot special economic zones in Guangdong and Fujian provinces. He was elected as a member of the Central Committee at the Party's Twelfth National Congress in September 1982.

Jiang Zemin served as mayor of Shanghai in 1985 and deputy secretary of the Shanghai Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China; in 1987 he was elected as a member of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee and secretary of the Shanghai Municipal Committee of the Communist Party of China; in 1989 he was elected as a member of the Standing Committee of the Political Bureau of the CPC Central Committee and general secretary of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China Chairman of the Military Council. The report pointed out that in the late 1980s and early 1990s, serious political turmoil occurred both at home and abroad. Jiang Zemin led the party's central leadership group to adhere to the four basic principles, safeguard national independence, dignity, security, and stability, and insist on economic construction as the center and reform. Opening up, opening up the situation of China's reform and opening up and socialist modernization.
President Xi Jinping expressed deep condolences for Jiang Zemin's death. He will turn his grief into strength and work together to build a modern socialist country and promote the great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation in an all-round way in accordance with the deployment of the 20th National Congress of the Communist Party of China. Officially announced the list of Comrade Jiang Zemin's funeral committee, with Xi Jinping serving as the chairman and members including Li Keqiang, Li Zhanshu, Wang Yang, Li Qiang, Zhao Leji, Wang Huning, Han Zheng, Cai Qi, Ding Xuexiang, Li Xi and other officials. The list also includes former President Hu Jintao, as well as Xia Baolong, Luo Huining, Dong Chee-hwa, Leung Chun-ying, He Yicheng, Ho Houhua, Li Jiachao and others from the Hong Kong and Macau system. Hong Kong Chief Executive Lee Kar-chao, Legislative Council Chairman Kwan-Yen Leung and all members of the Legislative Council yesterday expressed their deep condolences for the passing of former President Jiang Zemin and expressed deep condolences to Jiang Zemin's family. Macau Chief Executive Ho Iat Seng also expressed deep condolences to Jiang Zemin's passing away and extended sincere condolences to his family. Former Chief Executive Tung Chee-hwa, who has not been seen in public for a long time, also issued a statement expressing his deep condolences.
Flags fly at half-staff at Tiananmen Square and institutions stationed in Hong Kong and Macau
The funeral committee announced on Wednesday that in order to express the reverence and condolences of the whole party, the army and the people of all nationalities for Jiang Zemin, it is now decided to declare that the whole party, the whole army and the people of all nationalities in the country will end from the date of the release of the book to the date of the memorial meeting. Flags at Tiananmen Square, Xinhua Gate, the Great Hall of the People, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs, the Liaison Office of the Central People's Government in Hong Kong, Macau, and embassies and consulates abroad flew at half-mast to mourn. During this period, the Liaison Office of the Central People's Government in Hong Kong, Macau, and embassies and consulates abroad set up mourning halls to receive condolences from Hong Kong, Macao and the host countries. In addition, the main government buildings of the Hong Kong government, the Legislative Council and the judiciary will fly their flags at half-staff (including national flags and regional flags) from today. In addition, in accordance with Chinese practice, foreign governments, political parties and friendly people are not invited to send delegations or representatives to China to participate in mourning activities.
